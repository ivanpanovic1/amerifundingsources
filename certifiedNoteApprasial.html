Certified Note Apprasial

Why a note or cash flow appraisal?

For the following (and more) reasons:
  • To save you or your clients from overpaying taxes. When a client needs to sell part or all of a note for cash, s/he may want an independent source to set a price on a note. The math is only a part of the valuation process.
  • A note in an estate being probated may need to be appraised. Notes invested in a pension/profit-sharing plan may need annual valuations.
  • A client may be receiving payments from a personal injury settlement.
  • Perhaps one party in a divorce settlement accepted a note as his or her share.

What determines the value of a cash flow or a note?

People need or want access to their cash. Sometimes they have a serious need to pay off credit cards, finance long-term medical care, or to settle a divorce. Other times, they simply have a desire to purchase a dream home, take a vacation, buy a new car or boat, finance a wedding, or start a business, for example. In some cases, people want access to their cash just for peace of mind. They no longer want to worry about liquidity issues, collection hassles, or the financial strength of the person who owes the debt.

INTEREST OR YIELD

Many considerations, such as:
  • Equity in property securing the note
  • Payment history of payer
  • Priority of debt
  • Type of property
  • Condition of property
  • Note terms
  • Credit of payer
  • Property location

and more.

For a more complete explanation, read the article, "Determining the Value of a Note"

Who needs a note or cash flow appraisal?

Here's a short list:
  • IRS
  • Banks
  • Inheritance tax people
  • Divorce attorneys
  • Business dissolutions
  • Family members
  • Partnership contributions
  • Litigants
  • CPAs
  • Probates and estates
  • Charitable contributions
  • Pension, profit sharing plans, IRAs

and more.

What are some note appraisal situations?

Some examples:
Notes:
  • Secured or unsecured notes
  • Business notes without real estate
  • Seller carryback notes from sale of real estate
  • Seller carryback notes from sale of a mobile home in a rental park
Cash flows:
  • Leases - commercial and residential
  • Judgments
  • Royalty payments
  • Inheritances
  • Workman's comp awards
  • Lottery winnings / structured settlements...and more.

What does a note appraisal cost?

Really, it depends on the amount of time involved. For small notes, the fee is normally in the $300 to $400 range. Complex situations such as cash-flow valuations, unsecured notes, business notes (w/o RE) leases, etc. take more time, so the fee will be more

What are the qualifications of a note / cash flow appraiser?

Ideally, a person who evaluates notes and cash flows should have some or all of the following qualifications:
  • Must be a part of the marketplace for privately held notes and cash flows
  • Should have many years of experience in the discounted cash flow market
  • Should have recognized standing in the note and cash flow business
  • Must understand all types of notes and their securitizations
  • Must be able to clearly backup a value judgment
  • Must understand the math of present and future values
  • Should have credentials to qualify as an expert witness
  • Should be an established writer and speaker in the field
  • Very few people in America have the proper qualifications and experience to appraise notes.
